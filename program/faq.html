Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about admission decisions, passing grades, exchange programs, visas, and English test options.

What is the passing grade required for admission?

Passing (threshold) scores for university admission are set annually by the Ministry of Education and Science of the Kyrgyz Republic. Please check the current year’s official thresholds before applying.

How long does it take to get an admission decision?

It depends on the official procedure, but usually it takes about two weeks after submitting your documents.

What is an exchange program / credit mobility?

An exchange (credit mobility) is a period of study or training at a partner university for one semester or up to one academic year, with credits transferred back to AIU.

Do international students need a visa to study at AIU?

At Manas International Airport (Bishkek), incoming students can obtain a 1-month or 3-month student visa for living and studying in the Kyrgyz Republic. The International Relations Office (IRO) assists with visa matters.

Which English tests are accepted for international applicants?

AIU conducts its own English test; TOEFL, IELTS, and Duolingo scores are also accepted. Program-specific requirements may apply.

Who can apply for Erasmus+ outgoing mobility?

Selection considers eligibility, academic excellence, motivation, and English proficiency. Typical criteria include GPA 87+, English C1+, and not being in the first or last semester; prior KA171 grantees are not eligible. See current calls for any updates.

Sources: official AIU FAQ and IRO pages (content and availability may vary by year). If any policy changes, the most recent announcement on AIU websites prevails.
