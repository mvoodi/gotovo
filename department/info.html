About the CS Department

What Computer Science is, what we teach, and how students learn through theory, practice, and labs.

#overview #curriculum #labs

What is Computer Science?

Computers and the Internet are having a great impact on human life and on society. Our lives, starting in the second half of the twentieth century, have become computerized “at the speed of light.” We now live in times referred to as the computer age, the information age, or the Internet age.

The Computer Engineering Department offers a unique opportunity for bright young minds to excel in computer science, computer software, and computer hardware.

Curriculum Overview

Students begin with introductory courses in procedural and object-oriented programming and algorithm design. These lay the foundations for the vital intermediate subjects such as data structures and algorithm analysis, operating systems, programming languages, databases, computer networks, and the Internet.

Students also take courses in math, science, and engineering to prepare for more challenging coursework and projects ahead. In subsequent years, students take advanced and specialized subjects within Computer Engineering and across other engineering departments.

  • Programming fundamentals (procedural & OOP) and algorithm design
  • Core CS: data structures, OS, PL, databases, networks
  • Math & science foundation for engineering
  • Advanced electives and specialization in years 3–5
  • Senior design project in the final year
